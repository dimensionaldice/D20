The Origin of Dimensional Dice

Before Dimensional Dice became a name whispered at game tables, it was a legend of the Dragon of Fate, Kaelthoryn. Unlike his kin, Kaelthoryn hoarded not gold, but possibilities. To safeguard the infinite paths of destiny, he shattered a crystal of pure cosmic essence into seven shapes, each embodying a different aspect of story and chance.

The Seven Dice of Fate

  • D4 — The Spark of Beginnings. Every tale must start somewhere, sharp and radiant as the first strike of inspiration.
  • D6 — The Balance of Struggle. Conflict and resolution, order and chaos, the even dice of mortal trials.
  • D8 — The Blade of Conflict. Fate cuts sharpest in battle, where choices are swift and outcomes decisive.
  • D10s — The Weavers of Fortune. One die for the mortal path, one for eternity, spinning probability into endless patterns.
  • D12 — The March of Time. The cycles of days, months, and years, reminding adventurers that glory and ruin are bound to the ticking of the cosmos.
  • D20 — The Gate of Destiny. The dragon’s heart itself, a die of ultimate chance, where triumph and disaster balance on a single roll.
  • D100 — The Infinite Paths. Every roll hints at an uncharted story, countless destinies awaiting the brave.

From these seven forms came stories, and from stories, worlds. The first artisans of Dimensional Dice claimed they had uncovered fragments of Kaelthoryn’s hoard and sought to share them with mortals—not as shackles of fate, but as tools of creation.

Thus the company was born, with the dragon encircling the eternal d20 as its sigil. For every set forged carries a spark of Kaelthoryn’s gift: the power to open doors, shape tales, and remind us that every roll is more than chance—it is the heartbeat of adventure.
